# Business Discovery - School Management
## 03. User Personas

Version: 1.0
Status: In Progress
Last Updated: 2026-07-12

---

## SUMMARY

The School Management platform serves four primary user personas: Administrator, Teacher, Student, and Parent. Each has distinct goals, workflows, and system interaction patterns. Designing for varying technical literacy—from young students to non-technical parents—while maintaining appropriate security boundaries is essential.

---

## CONTEXT

Education platforms serve a diverse user base with overlapping but non-identical information needs. A typical institution has hundreds of students, dozens of teachers, hundreds of parents, and a small administrative team. UX must balance ease of use for less technical users with power and flexibility for administrators.

---

## PERSONAS

### Persona 1: School Administrator (Amina)
**Role:** Principal / Head of Administration
**Demographics:** 38-55 years, education management background, moderate to high technical proficiency
**Goals:** Maintain institutional compliance, oversee staff performance, manage admissions capacity, ensure parent communication
**Pain Points:** Disconnected systems for academics, finance, and HR; difficulty generating cross-functional reports; time-consuming compliance documentation
**Platform Needs:** Executive dashboard with KPIs, staff management and approval workflows, configurable reports, broadcast communication tools
**Key Tasks:** Approve enrollment and staff decisions, review academic trends, manage fee structures, coordinate emergency communications

### Persona 2: Teacher (James)
**Role:** Classroom Teacher
**Demographics:** 25-50 years, subject matter expert, variable technical proficiency
**Goals:** Deliver curriculum, track student progress, manage classroom administration, collaborate with colleagues and parents
**Pain Points:** Repetitive administrative tasks reducing teaching time; manual report card generation; inconsistent parent communication
**Platform Needs:** Gradebook with auto-calculation, attendance marking with parent notifications, lesson plan repository, direct messaging tools
**Key Tasks:** Mark daily attendance, record grades, distribute assignments, communicate progress to parents

### Persona 3: Student (Layla)
**Role:** Enrolled Learner
**Demographics:** 6-18 years, varying technical proficiency, mobile-first users
**Goals:** View schedule and assignments, check grades, submit work, communicate with teachers
**Pain Points:** Poor mobile access; unclear assignment expectations; lack of academic visibility; inconvenient fee and library processes
**Platform Needs:** Mobile-optimized dashboard, assignment submission, grade tracker, library search and renewal, online fee payment
**Key Tasks:** View daily schedule, submit assignments, check grades, reserve library books, pay fees

### Persona 4: Parent (David)
**Role:** Student Guardian
**Demographics:** 28-50 years, mixed technical proficiency, highly engaged
**Goals:** Monitor attendance and academic progress, pay fees, receive school announcements, communicate with teachers
**Pain Points:** Delayed attendance and academic notifications; inconvenient fee payment; difficulty reaching teachers directly
**Platform Needs:** Real-time attendance alerts, grade access, online fee payment, teacher messaging, school calendar notifications
**Key Tasks:** Review attendance records, view grades, pay fees online, message teachers, accept consent forms

---

## IMPLICATIONS FOR DESIGN

- **Role-Based Dashboards:** Each persona requires a tailored dashboard reflecting primary workflows and information needs.
- **Notification Strategy:** Different personas require different channels: SMS for parents, in-app for students, email for administrators.
- **Mobile Priority:** Students and parents are predominantly mobile users; teacher and administrator interfaces may be desktop-first but must remain responsive.
- **Onboarding:** Variable technical literacy requires contextual help, tutorials, and progressive disclosure of advanced features.
