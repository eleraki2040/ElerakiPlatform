# 1. Project Identity

## Project Name

Eleraki Platform

## First Application

Eleraki ERP

## Project Type

Business Platform

## Current Stage

Project Brain Construction

---

# 2. Vision

To build an engineering platform capable of representing real business behavior and serving as the foundation for multiple business systems.

The ERP system is only the first application built on top of this platform.

---

# 3. Mission

Create a reusable platform that enables the development of modern business applications through deep business understanding, solid engineering principles, and maintainable architecture.

---

# 4. Why Eleraki Exists

Most business systems start with:

- Database
- Screens
- CRUD Operations

Eleraki starts somewhere else.

It starts with understanding reality.

Only after understanding reality do we model business.

Only after modeling business do we design software.

Only after designing software do we write code.

---

# 5. Long-Term Vision

The platform should eventually support building systems such as:

- ERP
- Hospital Management
- School Management
- Factory Management
- CRM
- HR
- Hotel Management

without rebuilding the engineering foundation.

---

# 6. Project Scope

# 10. Business Thinking Model

Every business can be understood through:

Reality

↓

Business Events

↓

Business Rules

↓

Business Entities

↓

Business Documents

↓

Business State

The software must mirror this sequence.

---

# 11. Platform Strategy

The project is divided into two layers.

Layer One

Eleraki Framework

Reusable engineering components.

Layer Two

Business Applications

ERP is the first implementation.

Future applications should reuse the framework.

---

# 12. Architecture Strategy

The first architecture is:

Modular Monolith.

Migration to Microservices will happen only when justified by business and technical needs.

Premature complexity is rejected.

---

# 13. Documentation Strategy

The project must never depend on conversation history.

Knowledge belongs to documentation.

Every approved decision must be documented.

Documentation is considered part of the product.

---

# 14. Decision Making

Every engineering decision must answer:

What business problem does it solve?

Does it support the long-term vision?

Can it be understood years later?

If the answer is no, the decision must be reconsidered.

---

# 15. Quality Standards

Readable Architecture

Readable Documentation

Readable Code

Explicit Decisions

Minimal Complexity

High Maintainability

---

# 16. Definition of Success

Success is not measured by:

- Number of modules
- Number of screens
- Lines of code

Success is measured by:

- Clarity
- Maintainability
- Extensibility
- Engineering Quality
- Business Accuracy

---

# 17. Definition of Failure

The project is considered failing when:

Technology drives business decisions.

Architecture becomes unclear.

Documentation becomes outdated.

Code becomes difficult to understand.

Business rules become hidden inside implementation.
