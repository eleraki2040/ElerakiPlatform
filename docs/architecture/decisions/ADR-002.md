# ADR-002

Title:
Organization Unit Type is an Aggregate Root

Status:
Accepted

Date:
2026-07-05

Owner:
Architecture Team

---

# Context

The Organization Domain introduces the concept of Organization Unit Type to classify Organization Units.

Examples include:

- Company
- Branch
- Department
- Division
- Factory
- Warehouse
- Office
- Hospital
- Faculty
- Laboratory

A key architectural question emerged during domain modeling:

Should Organization Unit Type be treated as a simple lookup table, or as a full business entity with its own lifecycle?

This decision affects the domain model, repositories, application services, APIs, validation, and future extensibility.

---

# Decision

Organization Unit Type SHALL be modeled as an Aggregate Root within the Organization Domain.

It is an independent business entity with its own lifecycle, business rules, and identity.

It is NOT a lookup table.

It is NOT an enum.

It is NOT hardcoded.

---

# Rationale

Organization Unit Types have independent business behavior.

They can:

- Be created.
- Be modified.
- Be activated.
- Be deactivated.
- Be reused by multiple Organization Units.
- Be managed through business configuration.

These characteristics match the definition of an Aggregate Root in Domain-Driven Design.

---

# Consequences

Positive

- Supports business configuration.
- Eliminates hardcoded organizational structures.
- Enables future extensibility.
- Simplifies administration.
- Makes the platform industry-independent.
- Supports localization and customization.

Negative

- Slightly more complex implementation.
- Requires dedicated Repository.
- Requires dedicated Application Service.
- Requires dedicated validation rules.

These trade-offs are acceptable because long-term flexibility outweighs the additional implementation effort.

---

# Alternatives Considered

## Alternative 1

Represent Organization Unit Type as an enum.

Decision

Rejected.

Reason

Enums require recompilation whenever a new type is introduced.

They are unsuitable for configurable business platforms.

---

## Alternative 2

Represent Organization Unit Type as a lookup table.

Decision

Rejected.

Reason

Lookup tables usually have no business behavior, lifecycle, or ownership.

Organization Unit Type requires all of these.

---

## Alternative 3

Store type information directly inside Organization Unit.

Decision

Rejected.

Reason

This duplicates classification data and violates normalization and domain separation.

---

# Business Impact

Organizations can introduce new organizational structures without modifying source code.

Examples:

Existing types

- Branch
- Department
- Warehouse

Customer-defined types

- Research Center
- Innovation Hub
- Digital Office
- Regional Command
- Quality Laboratory

No software deployment is required.

---

# Domain Impact

Aggregate Roots within Organization Domain become:

- Organization
- Organization Unit Type

Organization Unit remains a domain entity that participates in the organizational hierarchy.

---

# Implementation Notes

Each Organization Unit references exactly one Organization Unit Type.

Organization Unit Types are managed independently.

Business validation must ensure that inactive types cannot be assigned to newly created Organization Units.
